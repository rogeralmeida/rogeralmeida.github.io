My Email Policy

When I was working for Atos, the company’s CEO started a zero email initiative. His aim was for the employees not to use email for internal communications. I remembered what I thought about it: “It’s impossible”. Now I believe he was right. This post explains how I deal with my professional email inbox.

Keep email closed

I don’t keep my email inbox open during working hours. I open it just when I have to. I also disabled my cellphone’s email notification. That prevents me from checking email whenever I see a new one arrives.

Schedule time to check email

I check my email twice a day, at 10:00 am and 04:00 pm. I have reminders in my calendar. That helps me organize my day and prevents me from being fooled by my weak memory.

Time box email time

As an adopter of the Pomodoro Technique I keep track of the time I spend checking email. What I noticed is that I need almost two pomodoros to read/respond/forward/archive my emails. That means I need almost two hours of my day to deal with emails. That was so true when I was working for ThoughtWorks. Now that I’m working for DiUS I’m trying not to sign in to so many mailing lists. My aim is to keep my email time in one pomodoro.

So far, so good

Up until now I’ve described what I have been doing for the past year. It has been working and I’ve noticed my productive increased since then. Before I was checking emails every time I changed windows. Now, I don’t suffer from that temptation. But there is one last step I wanna try…

Vacation Responder On

From now on I’m keeping my Vacation Responder on. This is my Vacation Responder reply:

Hi,

This is an auto-reply to inform you about my email policies:

  • I keep my email closed during working hours;
  • I disable cellphone notifications;
  • I only check emails twice a day at 10:00 am and 04:00 pm.

If you need to contact me, feel free to reach me out by phone (XXXX XXX XXX), Whatsapp or Viber.

I wanted to try it out for a long time, but never had the courage to actually do it. I’ll give it a try. Who knows, maybe it becomes a new post.
